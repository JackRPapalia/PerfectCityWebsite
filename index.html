Green Space

Community Fruit Gardens

🦋

Wildlife Corridors

🌿

Green Roofs Everywhere

Transit & Mobility

Every resident lives within 600 meters of a transit station. Dense transit hubs increase walkability and reduce car dependency.

⚡

Fully Electric Transit

Every bus, tram, and metro is 100% electric. No combustion engines on city streets. Modeled after Copenhagen's green transit network.

Like Copenhagen's metro — fully automated, driverless, running 24/7.

600m Station Promise

Every resident lives within 600 meters of a transit station. Dense transit hubs increase walkability and reduce car dependency.

Similar to Tokyo's station density — no resident left behind.
🚲

Cycling-First Design

Wide, smooth cycle paths with traffic lights timed at ~12 mph — the average cycling speed — so riders flow through the city without stopping at red lights.

Inspired by Copenhagen's 'Green Wave' cycling signal system.
🚶

Pedestrian Priority

Wide walkways, low speed limits, and vehicle-free zones throughout the city center. Safety-first street design puts walkers above all traffic.

Modeled on Barcelona's Superblocks — reclaiming streets for people.
Population & Equity

Built for Everyone

Thornburgh isn't built for the wealthy or the young — it's built for all 4 million residents. Equity isn't an afterthought; it's the foundation.

🏠

Affordable Housing

  • Government-aided rent assistance for qualifying tenants
  • Down payment assistance & tax incentives for first-time buyers
  • Adaptive reuse: converting vacant buildings and hotels into residential units
  • Increased density around major transit hubs — more housing where it matters
Inspired by Vienna's social housing model — 60% of residents live in subsidized housing.
🏙️

Mixed-Income Living

  • Vertically mixed buildings: market-rate and subsidized units in the same tower
  • No segregated 'poor neighborhoods' — wealth diversity by design
  • Shared amenities: parks, plazas, markets where all income levels interact daily
Like NYC's inclusionary zoning, but taken further — every building participates.
🏡

Zero Homelessness Goal

  • Government-funded jobs programs for financial instability
  • Housing-first approach with wraparound support services
  • Every district has transitional housing and mental health resources
Modeled after Finland's Housing First policy — the only EU country reducing homelessness.
🏥

Universal Healthcare

  • ~150 hospitals across 150 sq mi — one per square mile
  • Fair, timely access to high-quality care for every resident
  • Neighborhood clinics within walking distance of every home
Density exceeds even Singapore's world-leading healthcare access.
Architecture That Heals

Environmental Design

Modeled after Amsterdam's concentric canal layout, with architecture inspired by Prague's Gothic spires and Madrid's grand boulevards. What's above ground is beautiful—but what's beneath is optimized for long-term success.

🏗️

Carbon-Negative Skyscrapers

High-rises built with carbon-negative concrete that absorbs more CO₂ than it emits during production. Every skyscraper is a carbon sink.

🏰

Prague-Style Stone Houses

Lower residential areas feature stone brownstones and mid-rises inspired by Prague's historic Old Town — beautiful, durable, and thermally efficient.

🌲

Green Roofs & Vertical Forests

Every building features living green roofs. Taller structures incorporate vertical forests — like Milan's Bosco Verticale — with trees growing on every balcony.

☀️

Passive Solar Design

Glass roofs and large south-facing windows maximize natural light in Thornburgh's cold (45°F avg) climate, reducing heating needs through solar gain.

🌊

Flood-Resistant Design

Elevated ground floors, waterfront parks as natural buffers, and elevated skyscraper foundations protect against river flooding events.

💧

Water Recycling Systems

Every building recycles greywater from sinks and showers. Combined with filtered rainwater and river water, the city achieves near-zero water waste.

Future-Proofing

What Lies Beneath

The true optimization of Thornburgh is underground — water filtration, energy grids, waste processing, and food systems designed for circular, zero-waste operation.

Assuming climate change continues, populations shift, and extreme weather increases — Thornburgh is built to adapt, not collapse.

🌧️

Flooding & Water Stress

Waterfront parks act as natural flood buffers. Bioswales and permeable pavements manage stormwater. The hydroelectric dam includes flood-control gates. River water filtration ensures drought-resilient water supply.

🌍

Population Shifts & Climate Migration

High-density vertical living accommodates population growth without sprawl. Adaptive reuse policies convert vacant structures into housing rapidly. Transit-oriented density means new arrivals integrate without car dependency.

⚡

Energy Grid Resilience

Distributed microgrid architecture — solar, wind, and hydro across every district. No single point of failure. Battery storage in every building ensures 72-hour autonomy during grid disruptions.

🌾

Food Security

Urban farms, rooftop gardens, and community orchards produce a significant portion of the city's food locally. Indoor vertical farms operate year-round regardless of climate conditions. Free supermarkets eliminate food deserts.

🌡️

Biodiversity

Carbon-negative buildings absorb CO₂. Green roofs and urban forests moderate temperatures. The city's cold baseline (45°F avg) means heat islands are less severe, but tree canopy still provides cooling during warming summers.
